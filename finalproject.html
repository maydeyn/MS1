Research Question

How to educate people with normal vision about vision impairment?

Observation 1: Real World Experience - Dialogue in the Darkness

  • Location: Shenzhen, China
  • Date: 06/16/2019
  • Time: 7:00-8:30pm

In the summer of 2019, I stumbled upon a place called Dialogue in the Dark with my friend in Shenzhen when we were looking for interesting activities to go to. We did not know any details about this exhibition tour besides that it involves darkness. The fact that we went through this whole tour without much knowledge was crucial and it has made a huge impact on our experience.

There are 7 of us going through this experience together, we were greeted by a guide as soon as we stepped into the exhibition. Throughout the process, we had to learn how to handle daily life routines and navigate in daily environments in completely dark environment while interacting with strangers. It gets awhile to get used to, not just to adjust ourselves with darkness but also learning to identify the world through other senses, and to build trust with others. At first, I felt this experience makes me realize how much I appreciate my vision. However, as I spent more time in the darkness, I feel like I wasn’t any “weaker” without my vision, I was able to utilize my other senses and engage with others.

Towards the end of the tour, we sat down at a table and the guide asked if we had any questions. It wasn’t until then that I found out that our guide was blind. Everyone in the group thought he had a night vision goggles on, not only because he was familiar with the environment (which makes sense since he works there) but he was also able to tell our actions and positions in the darkness. He still has some perception of light but is unable to see anything clearly at all.

Dialogue in the Dark was a very unique experience for me - I went in “blind” - without any knowledge about this exhibition. It has educated and raised my awareness for diversity and inclusion for the vision impaired people. The experience helps us to overcome false sympathy, prejudices and stereotypes. In “Darkness” Interest replaces ignorance, and lack of understanding changes into empathy.”
