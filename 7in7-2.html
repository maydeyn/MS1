Question

How to create an immersive, unique yet educational experience about vision impairment for young adults?

Conceptualization

Inspired by the concept of Transmedia Storytelling - telling the same story in different forms that eventually emerge and become a whole and unique experience. I wanted to explore this concept with my research question by connecting physical and digital media. I believe this technique would be appealing to young adults since it encourages the use of multiple senses, technologies, and media all at once, letting the audiences to be explorative.
Keywords: exploration, collaboration, senses, immersive, experience, interactive

Development

This two-person interactive experience will be conducted in a park.
The physical prototype will include three parts:
  • A “blindfolding” glasses that resemble the look and feel of a regular sunglasses. The user will not be able to see anything clearly but will still have light perception (simulating legally blind).
  • An earpiece that is connected wirelessly to another device (mobile or computer).
The digital prototype will require a friend (someone who you can trust) to guide you: The goal here is to help your limited vision friend to navigate through and eventually exit the park.
    RULES:
  • No directional guidance (left, right, clock position, etc). You can only give hints related to smell or sound. Your blindfolded friend will have to navigate using only auditory and olfactory senses.
  • Two of you must be at least 10 yards apart at all times.
