Final Prototype


Reflection of the Semester

What are the specific questions you are attempting to answer in your prototypes?

  • How to make the medical procedures and process less intimidating for the general public?
  • How to repurpose a medical procedure (an eye exam) that creates a non-invasive environment and encourages self-exploration, but still follow the process structure of an eye exam?
  • Can the repurposed interaction that mimics an eye exam be further developed into a project that may promote better experiences for patients visiting optometrists?

What the five strengths of your project?

  • The uniqueness in terms of the concept and form, repurposing medical devices, diagnosis process, and medical language into a playful interaction with the user itself and poetry.
  • The aesthetic of the phoropter is eye-catching and generates a lot of initial interest from audiences.
  • The use of Poetry gives audiences room for interpretation and imagination.
  • Self-exploratory interactions
  • Non-invasive environment

What are the five most critical issues for your project?

  • Clarity on the interactive process
  • Meaning behind the poetry and how it is being generated?
  • What is being tracked through users' eyes?
  • Where can this installation be implemented to achieve my goal?
  • Incorporate more unique results for each individual

What can you do to address these issues, and to solidify the strengths?

  • Implementing technology that can identify emotions through eyes and facial expressions, then generate a poem based on the mood. This will clarify the diagnosis process.
  • Add an instructional interface for the users when they look into the phoropter to explain the interaction process.
  • Add a more sophisticated poetry API to generate appropriate poetry that matches users’ moods instead of AI-generated poems that may turn out gibberish.
  • Optometry schools, optometrists offices or hospitals may be appropriate locations for this installation. More scouting needs to be conducted.

What new questions do you have?

  • Is it possible to breakdown the medical language into a more approachable language for the general public?
  • Does everyone have negative experiences or feel intimidated by the medical procedures?
  • How do medical professionals feel when they interact with my project? Does the process make sense to them?

What questions need to be answered in order to create a proof of concept prototype?

  • How do the audience feel after the self-diagnosis process?
  • Does this resonates with people who have not visited an optometrist/used a phoropter/had an eye exam before?
  • Does the set up of AEye Poet reminds the audience of medical experiences or environment?
  • How do the audience respond to the poetry they receive? How do they interpret it?

How has this prototype allowed you to engage in your goals within this program? Does this feel like a possible future direction?

    This project is definitely something I’ve never done before. The implementation of new technologies that I am unfamiliar with and the process of connecting physical computing with real-time data was fun and challenging. I think this project can be developed into a much more sophisticated installation in the future. The current prototype feels like a scratch on the surface for what my goal is and I hope to develop this AEye Poet to a more complete and standalone project, and perhaps extend this concept into a series of work.
