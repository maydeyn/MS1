Question

How to reduce false sympathy and lack of understanding of people with disabilities?

Conceptualization

One of my favorite experience of learning about other people’s stories was through an exhibition called Museum of Broken Relationships:
It is a physical and virtual public space created with the sole purpose of treasuring and sharing your heartbreak stories and symbolic possessions. It is a museum about you, about us, about the ways we love and lose. At its core, the Museum is an ever-growing collection of items, each a memento of a relationship past, accompanied by a personal, yet anonymous story of its contributor. Unlike ‘destructive’ self-help instructions for recovery from grief and loss, the Museum offers the chance to overcome an emotional collapse through creativity - by contributing to its universal collection.”

Development

“I Wish They Knew” is a disabled community-driven museum space that exists in both physical and virtual space. It accepts objects with a personal story from all around the world anonymously. They can share anything - their pain and struggles; their inspirational stories; their moment of clarity - anything that they wish someone else knew about them, during the journey of life with disability.

Outcome


I used SketchUp to create a quick a museum space that will display the objects that has been sent. As you can see there is no limitation on what type of objects that can be put on display, as long as there is no personal information or photographs. Each object will have a label that indicates the name of the object, date of when the story occurred, and the story. It will also be available as audio.

Reflection

What I liked about this prototype:
  • Community-driven project, you can hear different stories from people all around the world
  • People can make emotional connections, be more understanding and replace sympathy with empathy.
    • Issues/future development:
  • Limited to English languages. Perhaps have translation services.
  • May take a long time to build up the project, including the selection process.
  • How often should the objects be updated?
